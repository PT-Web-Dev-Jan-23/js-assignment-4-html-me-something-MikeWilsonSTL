Five ✋ of my Favorite Hobbies

An exploration of HTML, CSS, and publishing through Git as well as how I like to spend my time.

Music

Might as well get the biggest one out of the way. I'm a professional musician who specializes in acoustic fingerstyle guitar. Over the years, however, I've ended up playing in nearly every major western genre. These days I play guitar in the hip-hop scene and I am constantly inspired by the talent I find myself surrounded with.

Magic

Before music became my primary focus I thought about pursuing magic profesionally long-term. I've done magic since I was about 8 years old and for a year or so I did it full-time. I specialized in street magic and improvisational magic. You wouldn't find me on stage but you'd find me at a bar or private event. These days I don't practice magic like I used to but I still have more than a few tricks up my sleeve.

Retro Game Enthusiast

I am a huge retro game/emulation nerd. You name it, I've tried it. I love the challenge of configuring settings, scraping metadata, and designing themes to create awesome UI/UX experiences. I also love the challenge of getting the emulators to run in ways they were designed but also in ways that they weren't. One of the things I plan to do in the future is to find a meaningful way to contribute to my favorite open-source emulators and emulator front-ends.

Parkour/Tumbling

I started training parkour in February of 2012 and it led me to somehow spending the next 4 years travelling across the country on a shoestring budget. Some of the best memories of my life were during this phase and it's a time I remember fondly. These days I throw a few flips when I can, but I'm more focused on developing my careers in coding and music.

Cooking/Bartending

Making food and drink is probably one of my favorite ways to show appreciation for the people who matter to me. I normally go for time-consuming projects like dry-aging steaks at home and infusing whiskeys and vodkas because I also love saving money and these things are way cheaper when you do them yourself.
